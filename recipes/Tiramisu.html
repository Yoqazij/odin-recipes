Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu, meaning "pick me up" in Italian, is a rich and creamy no-bake dessert made of layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, mascarpone cream, and dusted cocoa. It's elegant, indulgent, and surprisingly easy to make at home.

Ingredients

For the Cream:
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (240 ml) heavy cream, cold
  • 8 oz (225 g) mascarpone cheese, room temperature
For Assembly:
  • 1 1/2 cups (360 ml) strong brewed coffee or espresso, cooled1 1/2 cups (360 ml) strong brewed coffee or espresso, cooled
  • 3 tbsp coffee liqueur (optional, e.g., Kahl√∫a or Marsala wine)
  • 1 package (7 oz / 200 g) ladyfinger cookies (Savoiardi)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Steps

  1. Make Cream

     Whisk egg yolks + sugar over simmering water until thick. Cool. Whip cream to stiff peaks, fold in mascarpone, then combine with yolk mixture.

  2. Make Coffee Dip

     Mix brewed coffee with liqueur (optional). Let cool.

  3. Assemble

     Quickly dip ladyfingers in coffee. Layer in a dish. Add half the cream. Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and cream.

  4. Chill

     Refrigerate for at least 6 hours (or overnight).

  5. Serve

     Dust with cocoa powder and add chocolate shavings if you like. Enjoy!
